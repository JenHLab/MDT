Miami-Dade County Metrobus System

Background



The public transportation system in the county is managed by the Miami-Dade Transit (MDT) agency. MDT can trace its origins back to 1960 when County Commission passed an ordinance to unify the different transit operations into one countywide service.

MDT offers bus services (Metrobus), rail services (Metrorail and Metromover), and Paratransit services, which is a door to door service for residents with disabilities.

The map on the right shows the network of metromover stations compared to metrobus stations.

Annual ridership of public transportation in the county has steadily increased since 2003, reaching a record high ridership of 114,208,800 in 2008. During the recession, ridership decreased as a result of job losses. However, with the improvement of the economy in 2010, ridership is slowly increasing.

Metrobus



The metrobus is the most popular form of public transportation for Miami-Dade residents. This can be attributed to the fact that it is the only public transportation network available throughout the county.

The metrobus network covers area from Miami Beach to west Miami-Dade and from Key Largo to the Broward County line. The metrobus has more than 90 routes and a fleet of over 600 buses.


Ridership Preferences in Miami-Dade County (October, 2015)

Ridership preferences show that the metrobus is mostly used during Monday through Friday. This provides insight that metrobus is heavily used by commuters.

In fact, annual metrobus ridership data shows how during the economic recession ridership sharply declined. It was not until the Miami-Dade economy started improving in 2010, that metrobus ridership began to increase.

Metrobus Annual Ridership in Miami-Dade County

The most popular metrobus route is Route S with an average monthly ridership of more than 350,000 riders since 2009. Route S starts at the Government Center in Downtown Miami, crosses to Miami Beach over the MacArthur Causeway and transits Collins Avenue until reaching Aventura Boulevard.

Metrobus Route Ridership in Miami-Dade County

Route 11 is the second most popular route stretching from Downtown Miami west to 107th Avenue on Flagler St and ending at FIU Modesto Maidique Campus. Route 11 has a monthly ridership average of over 300,000 riders.

Route L, with an average of more than 250,00 riders, is the third most ppular route. It starts at the Miami Beach Convention Center, goes north on Collins Avenue and heads west on the 79 St Causeway to the Hialeah Metrorail Station stopping at the Northside Metrorail Station, Amtrak Passenger Terminal, and Tri-Rail Metrorail Station.

All other routes average as little as 1,000 riders per month up to 200,000. The least popular bus routes are Route 254, Route 500, Route 354 and Route 202.

The Challenge



MDT faces numerous challenges in the transportation system infrastructure. Trends in population growth and rising prices in the real estate market are making new, affordable constructions in the southwestern areas of the county appealing for residents. With the metrobus being the only countywide public transportation system for commuters and residents alike, the challenge for MDT is to make sure that in the short term, the metrobus can prove to be a feasible alternative to commuters.

Yet, it is precisely the southwestern portions of the county that have an even more limited transportation infrastructure. In the next couple of years, Miami-Dade commuters can expect an increase in traffic congestion particularly in the areas of Kendall, Cutler Ridge, and Homestead, and on main highways like the Turnpike between Florida City and the City of Miami and the Palmetto (SR-826).
